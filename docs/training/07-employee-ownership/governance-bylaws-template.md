# Governance & Bylaws Template

> **Audience:** Founders, stewards, and counsel adapting Plan-Examiner's
> employee-ownership model to a specific legal entity and jurisdiction.
>
> **Status:** This is a **template**, not a finished legal document.
> Pair it with counsel licensed in your jurisdiction. Nothing here is
> legal, tax, or investment advice.

The template captures the *governance principles* we want preserved
across whatever legal vehicle is chosen — worker cooperative, ESOP,
employee-ownership trust, professionally-licensed entity, or worker-
owned LLC. The exact mechanism for translating a principle into law
varies by vehicle.

Bracketed `[choices]` indicate values to be set when adopting.

---

## Article I — Name and purpose

**§1.1 Name.** The organization shall be known as `[legal entity name]`,
operating under the brand "Plan-Examiner."

**§1.2 Purpose.** The organization exists to provide AI-assisted
building-plan compliance review, to maintain and extend the open-source
Plan-Examiner codebase, and to do so in a manner that:

1. Preserves the architectural privacy promise that plans never leave
   the user's browser;
2. Operates the rule engine deterministically and auditably;
3. Distributes ownership and decision-making among the people who
   perform the work;
4. Sustains the work over a horizon measured in decades, not quarters.

**§1.3 No surrender of mission.** Article I may be amended only by
**supermajority** vote per Article VIII.

---

## Article II — Membership and ownership

**§2.1 Eligibility.** A person becomes eligible for ownership upon:

(a) being employed by the organization for at least `[90]` days, and

(b) successfully completing the milestones described in the
    [New-Hire 30/60/90-Day Onboarding](../04-onboarding/new-hire-onboarding.md).

**§2.2 Admission.** An eligible employee is admitted as an owner upon
a **simple majority** vote of owners present at a duly noticed meeting.
Absence of a unanimous-consent requirement is intentional and may not
be amended into the bylaws.

**§2.3 Owner classes.** Ownership is **single-class**. There are no
preferred shares, no super-voting shares, and no perpetual founder
rights.

**§2.4 Voting.** Each owner has **one vote** on each matter before the
owners. Proxy voting is permitted only with written designation in
advance.

**§2.5 Termination of ownership.**

(a) **Voluntary withdrawal.** An owner may withdraw upon written
    notice. Buy-out per Article VII.

(b) **Withdrawal upon departure.** Cessation of active employment for
    more than `[12]` months automatically terminates ownership unless
    the owner is on a protected leave or has been retained as an
    advisor by supermajority vote.

(c) **Removal for cause.** An owner may be removed for cause by
    supermajority vote following written notice of the alleged cause
    and a meaningful opportunity to respond. Cause is defined as
    sustained material breach of the [Code of Conduct](../../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md),
    fraud, or behavior that materially endangers the organization or
    its members.

**§2.6 Non-discrimination.** Eligibility, admission, voting, removal,
compensation, and buy-out shall not be conditioned on race, color,
religion, national origin, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation,
age, disability, marital status, family responsibilities, veteran
status, or any other protected category.

---

## Article III — Governance bodies

**§3.1 The Owners.** The owners are the supreme governance body. All
matters reserved to them by Articles VII and VIII are theirs alone.

**§3.2 The Steward Council.** A council of `[5–9]` stewards, each
holding one of the domains listed in the
[Executive Leadership Handbook §2](../05-executive/executive-leadership-handbook.md#2-the-leadership-domains).

(a) Stewards are nominated by any owner, confirmed by majority vote of
    owners present at a duly noticed meeting.

(b) Stewards serve `[2-year]` rotating terms, with no term limit, but
    subject to recall at any time by **majority** vote of owners
    present.

(c) The Steward Council may make decisions reserved to it by Article
    IV without further owner vote.

**§3.3 The Executive Officer (optional).** The owners may, by majority
vote, designate one steward as the executive officer for external
purposes (signature on contracts, reporting to government agencies).
The executive officer holds no governance powers beyond those of any
other steward.

**§3.4 Trainer Council.** Curriculum changes for the
[trainer track](../03-trainer/) are decided by a council of any three
Level 300 trainers. The Steward Council does not override trainer
council decisions absent a clear ethical or legal concern.

---

## Article IV — Decisions reserved to the Steward Council

The following decisions are made by the Steward Council, requiring
consent of `[a majority of stewards]`:

- Operational runbooks and their revisions;
- Vendor selections under threshold `[$X]`;
- Hiring and onboarding pipeline operations;
- Quality-sampling regimes;
- Day-to-day pricing within published bands.

Decisions outside this scope escalate to the Owners.

---

## Article V — Decisions reserved to the Owners

The following decisions are made by the Owners by **simple majority**
of those present, with quorum of `[50%+1]` of all owners:

- Confirmation of stewards;
- Annual budget approval;
- Annual community-contribution recipient;
- Adoption of new owners under Article II;
- Pricing changes that exceed the published bands;
- Material vendor changes above threshold `[$X]`;
- Any matter referred to them by the Steward Council;
- Any matter on which any owner has invoked the
  [any-owner-can-demand-a-vote rule](../05-executive/executive-leadership-handbook.md#73-the-any-owner-can-demand-a-vote-rule).

---

## Article VI — Supermajority decisions

The following decisions require a **two-thirds supermajority** of all
owners (not merely those present):

**§6.1 Architectural & licensing.**

(a) Any change that would route plan data through servers controlled
    by the organization;

(b) Any closing of source code that is open source on the date of
    these bylaws;

(c) Adoption of an "open core" model in which premium features sit
    behind closed code.

**§6.2 Ownership & control.**

(d) Sale of the organization or any equivalent change of control;

(e) Issuance of any equity or equity-equivalent instrument to a
    non-employee party;

(f) Acceptance of an outside investor (limited exceptions for
    non-equity loans and grants under §6.3).

**§6.3 Financial integrity.**

(g) Material reductions in the profit-sharing share of net profit
    (see [equity guide](profit-sharing-and-equity.md));

(h) Single-customer revenue concentration above `[30%]` in any
    rolling 12 months — if reached, requires explicit supermajority
    *renewal*.

**§6.4 Constitutional.**

(i) Amendments to Articles I, II, VI, VII, VIII;

(j) Removal of an owner for cause under §2.5(c);

(k) Dissolution of the organization.

## Article VII — Compensation, profit-sharing, and equity

**§7.1 Salary.** Salary is set by role per the published compensation
ladder ([Hiring Playbook §5](../04-onboarding/hiring-team-playbook.md#5-promotion-ladder)).
Pay parity by role is a binding policy.

**§7.2 Profit-sharing.** Net profit, after retention of the
runway-floor reserves and the reinvestment percentage required by
[Sustainability §2.3](../06-operations/sustainability-and-stewardship.md#23-reinvestment),
is distributed to owners under the formula in the
[profit-sharing & equity guide](profit-sharing-and-equity.md).

**§7.3 Buy-out on termination of ownership.** Withdrawing or removed
owners are bought out at the formula and schedule defined in the
[profit-sharing & equity guide](profit-sharing-and-equity.md).

**§7.4 Reinvestment floor.** No less than `[X%]` of annual net profit
is reinvested in the organization.

**§7.5 Community-contribution floor.** No less than `[Y%]` of annual
net profit is contributed to the recipient elected by the Owners
under Article V.

---

## Article VIII — Amendments

**§8.1 Standard amendments.** Articles III, IV, V, and VII may be
amended by **simple majority** vote of Owners at a duly noticed
meeting.

**§8.2 Constitutional amendments.** Articles I, II, VI, and VIII may
be amended only by **two-thirds supermajority** of all Owners (not
merely those present), at a meeting noticed at least `[30]` days in
advance with the proposed amendment text.

**§8.3 No-surprise rule.** Any amendment proposal must be circulated
in writing at least `[14]` days before the vote. An amendment voted
without proper notice is void.

---

## Article IX — Meetings

**§9.1 Quarterly owners' meetings.** The Owners shall meet at least
quarterly. Notice of `[14]` days minimum. Hybrid in-person/virtual
attendance is supported.

**§9.2 Annual general meeting.** Once per year, on a fixed cadence.
Bylaws review, compensation review, mission letter, community
contribution.

**§9.3 Emergency meetings.** Any `[three]` owners or the Steward
Council may call an emergency meeting on `[72]` hours' notice for
matters that cannot wait for the regular cadence.

**§9.4 Minutes.** All meetings produce written minutes, signed by
two owners (one not on the Steward Council), and archived in a
location accessible to all owners.

**§9.5 Quorum.** `[50%+1]` of all Owners constitutes a quorum, except
where a higher threshold is required by these bylaws.

---

## Article X — Dispute resolution

**§10.1 Internal first.** Disputes among owners or between owners and
the organization shall be raised through the
[escalation path](../05-executive/executive-leadership-handbook.md#71-the-escalation-path)
before any external proceeding.

**§10.2 Mediation.** Disputes that survive internal escalation shall
first attempt **mediation** with a mutually agreeable mediator before
arbitration or litigation.

**§10.3 No retaliation.** No owner shall be retaliated against for
raising a dispute in good faith.

---

## Article XI — Dissolution

**§11.1 Procedure.** Dissolution requires supermajority under
Article VI. The dissolution plan must include treatment of:

- Ongoing client engagements;
- Open-source codebase stewardship (preferred: transfer to a
  recognized foundation or successor cooperative);
- Owner buy-out per the equity guide;
- Charitable distribution of any residual after liabilities, per the
  community-contribution policy.

**§11.2 No private benefit.** No part of the dissolution may direct
disproportionate value to founders, executives, or any subset of
owners.

## Adoption checklist

When adopting these bylaws to a specific legal entity:

- [ ] Replace bracketed `[values]` with concrete choices.
- [ ] Map each Article to the equivalent provisions in your chosen
      legal vehicle (cooperative bylaws / ESOP plan documents /
      operating agreement).
- [ ] Confirm all anti-discrimination, employment, and securities
      requirements with counsel.
- [ ] Confirm tax structure with an accountant familiar with your
      vehicle.
- [ ] Sign with all founding owners.
- [ ] Archive the executed copy and link it from this template.
