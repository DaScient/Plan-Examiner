# Plan-Examiner — Training & Knowledge-Sharing Hub

> *"Build the people, and the people will build the product."*

Welcome to the Plan-Examiner **train-the-trainer** library. These materials are
designed to support every level of business execution — from a brand-new staff
member opening the app for the first time, to the team that hires and onboards
the next generation of plan reviewers, to the executives and worker-owners who
steward the company itself.

This library is **license-aligned with the project** ([MIT](../../LICENSE)) and
is meant to be forked, taped to a whiteboard, projected at a workshop, or
walked through in a living-room cooperative meeting. Use it.

---

## How this library is organized

```
docs/training/
├── 01-foundations/       What Plan-Examiner is and why it exists
├── 02-end-user/          Day-to-day operator skill-building
├── 03-trainer/           For people who teach the operators
├── 04-onboarding/        For people who hire the operators
├── 05-executive/         For people who lead the company
├── 06-operations/        For people who run and maintain the company
└── 07-employee-ownership/ For all of us — because we own this together
```

Every track is **self-contained** but cross-references the others. A staff
trainer can read the trainer track without first reading the executive track,
and vice versa.

## Conventions used in this library

- **Callouts** use the standard markdown `>` blockquote.
- **Time-boxes** for exercises are shown as `⏱ 15 min`.
- **Skill levels** are shown as `Level 100` (intro), `Level 200` (working), `Level 300` (mastery).
- **Roles** are shown in **bold** (e.g., **Reviewer**, **Trainer**, **Steward**, **Owner-Member**).
- **Code/UI elements** use `inline code`. Real product copy is quoted verbatim where it appears in [README.md](../../README.md).

> **A note on truth:** Where these materials describe Plan-Examiner's behavior,
> they are aligned with the public README. Where they describe operations,
> governance, or ownership structure, they are **templates and recommended
> practices** that any adopting organization should adapt to its own legal,
> jurisdictional, and cultural context. Nothing here is legal, tax, or
> investment advice.

## How to contribute to this library

1. Pick the track that fits your change.
2. Keep voice consistent: plain English, second-person ("you"), short
   sentences, generous tables, examples before abstractions.
3. Cross-link with relative paths so the docs work both on GitHub and on the
   GitHub Pages site.
4. Open a PR using the [PR template](../../.github/pull_request_template.md).
   In the description, name the **track** and **audience** for your change.

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](../../CONTRIBUTING.md) for the broader contribution
guidelines, the [Code of Conduct](../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md), and
[SOURCING.md](../../SOURCING.md) for any code-text excerpts.
