# Sustainability & Stewardship Guide

> **Audience:** Stewards and owners thinking about the *long* run —
> a decade or more — for Plan-Examiner the company.
>
> **Outcome:** Practical commitments and habits that make this an
> organization worth staying in, and worth handing to the next cohort
> of reviewers.

Sustainability here means three things at once:

1. **Human sustainability** — people who do this work for years without
   being burned out or burned through.
2. **Financial sustainability** — a business that pays its people well,
   reinvests, and survives recessions and slow quarters.
3. **Mission sustainability** — privacy, deterministic review, and
   open-source rule packs preserved across leadership changes.

---

## 1. Human sustainability

### 1.1 Workload commitments
- We staff to **70% of nominal capacity**, leaving 30% for surprises,
  training, contributions, and rest.
- We do not normalize overtime. A reviewer staying late twice a week
  is a system failure that the operations steward investigates.
- "Crunch" is not a strategy. If demand exceeds capacity, the
  steward council's first move is to slow intake, not lengthen days.

### 1.2 Leave and rest
- Minimum **4 weeks** of paid time off, encouraged.
- No "use-it-or-lose-it" pressure: a reviewer who hasn't taken at
  least one full week off by mid-year gets a polite check-in.
- One "fallow week" per year per reviewer: no client work, used for
  contribution, learning, or genuinely nothing. Pay continues.

### 1.3 Career arcs we support
Not every senior person wants to manage. Our promotion ladder explicitly
includes *individual-contributor* tracks (rule-pack author, master
reviewer, trainer) that compensate at parity with steward roles. See
[Hiring Playbook §5](../04-onboarding/hiring-team-playbook.md#5-promotion-ladder).

### 1.4 Mental-health and accessibility commitments
- Equipment that suits the reviewer (large monitors, ergonomic chairs,
  software for accessibility) is provided, no questions asked.
- Anyone may request a workload pause due to medical or personal
  reasons; we accommodate within policy and protect privacy.
- We measure inclusion and equity in hiring (see [Hiring Playbook §7](../04-onboarding/hiring-team-playbook.md#7-equity-and-inclusion-in-our-hiring))
  and re-measure annually.

### 1.5 Conflict and safety
- The [Code of Conduct](../../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) governs behavior.
- Stewards are trained in conflict mediation. The
  [escalation path](../05-executive/executive-leadership-handbook.md#71-the-escalation-path)
  is published.

## 2. Financial sustainability

### 2.1 Cash discipline
- We hold **at least 6 months of operating cash** as a floor; the
  finance steward reports the runway monthly to all owners.
- Profit-sharing distributions are made *after* runway requirements
  are met (see [profit-sharing guide](../07-employee-ownership/profit-sharing-and-equity.md)).

### 2.2 Pricing
- Pricing covers full loaded cost (people + tooling + reasonable
  overhead) plus a margin sized for resilience, not maximization.
- We publish our pricing. We do not negotiate quietly. Any
  exception requires [rung 4](../05-executive/executive-leadership-handbook.md#4-decision-rights-ladder).

### 2.3 Reinvestment
At least a fixed percentage of net profit (specified in the
[bylaws](../07-employee-ownership/governance-bylaws-template.md))
is reinvested annually into:
- Rule-pack expansion (new jurisdictions, new editions)
- Training infrastructure (workshops, materials, trainer cohorts)
- Tooling that materially improves reviewer ergonomics or quality

We do **not** reinvest into growth-at-any-cost, marketing budgets that
exceed our throughput, or speculative product lines. Boring is fine.

### 2.4 Recession behavior
The first move in a downturn is **not** layoffs. In order:
1. Pause discretionary spend
2. Pause profit-sharing distributions (with owner vote)
3. Reduce hours / pay across the team (transparently, distributively)
4. Then, only if necessary, reduce headcount via the
   [bylaws-defined process](../07-employee-ownership/governance-bylaws-template.md)

### 2.5 Vendor and provider concentration
We avoid being concentrated in any one vendor or LLM provider. The
"BYO-key" architecture is part of this — clients can choose providers
that meet their procurement needs without us being on the hook.

## 3. Mission sustainability

The mission is fragile across leadership changes. These commitments
make it durable.

### 3.1 The privacy promise is in the architecture
Plan-Examiner's most important product property — *plans never leave
the browser* — is enforced by the architecture, not by policy. A future
leader cannot quietly walk it back without rewriting code, which is a
public, reviewable act.

The bylaws additionally require a **supermajority** to introduce any
server-side processing of plan data.

### 3.2 Open-source covenant
- The codebase is MIT-licensed.
- Rule packs we author are open source.
- We do not "open core" — premium features behind closed code are
  forbidden by the bylaws.

### 3.3 Independence from any one customer
We will not allow any single customer to exceed a published share of
revenue (typical default: **30%**). This protects reviewer judgment from
commercial pressure.

### 3.4 The trainer cascade
The fastest way to lose institutional knowledge is to centralize it.
We require:
- At least **two qualified trainers per Level** at all times.
- Each trainer commits to one cohort per year.
- Curriculum changes go through the [trainer council](../03-trainer/workshop-curriculum.md#curriculum-maintenance).

### 3.5 The annual mission letter
Once a year, the mission steward writes a public letter to all
owners covering:
- What we did this year and why
- What we did *not* do, and why we declined
- How privacy posture changed (better, worse, the same)
- Risks we are tracking
- What we'd love help with next year

The letter is archived. Future leaders can read 10 years of letters and
see the through-line.

## 4. Environmental and social stewardship

We are a small, software-mostly business; our environmental and social
footprint is correspondingly small but not zero.

### 4.1 Compute and energy
- LLM compute is opt-in and per-reviewer; we do not encourage
  unnecessary calls.
- We **prefer providers with credible decarbonization commitments**
  when other factors are equal.
- We do not run server fleets; the static-site architecture has a
  near-zero idle cost.

### 4.2 Travel
- Workshops are **default-virtual**.
- Travel for client work or industry events is approved per trip.
- The annual owners' meeting is hybrid; in-person is for those who
  want it, never required.

### 4.3 Community contribution
- We donate a fixed percentage of net profit (specified in bylaws)
  to building-code education, accessibility advocacy, or
  jurisdictional capacity-building each year, with the recipient
  chosen by an annual owners' vote.
- We sponsor at least one **scholarship workshop** per year — a
  Level 100 or 200 cohort attended free by participants from
  under-represented groups in the AEC industry.

### 4.4 Suppliers
We prefer suppliers that pay living wages, support remote work, and
have credible privacy postures. We re-evaluate annually.

## 5. The "10-year question"

Every steward, once a year, answers this question in writing for the
owners' meeting:

> *"What is one decision we are making today that we will, in 10 years,
> wish we had made differently? And what would the alternative look
> like?"*

The answers are not promises; they are exercises in long-horizon
thinking. Past answers should be re-read at the next year's meeting.
Patterns across years tell us what we keep failing to do.

## 6. Sustainability KPIs

A short list. Track these alongside the operational KPIs.

| KPI | Target | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Median PTO taken / eligible | ≥80% | Annual |
| Sustained-overtime incidents | 0 | Monthly |
| Cash runway | ≥6 months | Monthly |
| Single-customer revenue share | ≤30% | Quarterly |
| Trainer count per level | ≥2 | Quarterly |
| Reinvestment % of net profit | per bylaws | Annual |
| Community-contribution % of net profit | per bylaws | Annual |
| Scholarship workshops delivered | ≥1 | Annual |
| Anonymous "would-recommend" survey | ≥80% yes | Annual |
